4.1

What Makes a Vowel 'Long'?

When a vowel is long, it says its name — the same sound it makes when you recite the alphabet.

Long A says "ay."  Long E says "ee."  Long I says "eye."  Long O says "oh."  Long U says "you."

The Big Idea: In Chapter 3, you learned that CVC words contain a short vowel. In this chapter you will learn the two main patterns that make a vowel go long: the Silent E and Vowel Teams. Together, these unlock thousands of new words.
Two Patterns

How Long Vowels Are Created

1Silent E (Magic E): A final silent E flips the previous vowel from short to long.  cap → cape  |  pin → pine  |  hop → hope
2Vowel Teams: Two vowels side by side often team up to produce one long sound.  rain, feet, boat, moon

4.2 — Pattern 1

The Silent E — Magic E

A silent E at the end of a word reaches back over the consonant and forces the previous vowel to say its long sound. The E itself is completely silent.

💡 Think of it as a light switch: the silent E flips the vowel from short to long — without making any sound itself.

4.3 — Pattern 2

Vowel Teams

When two vowels sit side by side, they often team up to produce one long vowel sound.

Memory Rule: "When two vowels go walking, the first one does the talking."
The first vowel says its long name. The second is usually silent.
